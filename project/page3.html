Halvatic Origins

Halva (sometimes spelled names halawa, haleweh, halava, helava, helva, halwa, aluva, chalva, chałwa, alva....) is a dessert served (usually after Shawarma) in the Middle East and adjacent regions (Eastern Europe, Northeast Africa, etc). Halawa "derived from the Arabic: حلوى ḥalwá, a sweet confection.[2] The Arabic root حلو ḥelw means "sweet" (not going to lie, this is straight from Wikipedia). I'm not going to pretend that Halva is nearly as awesome as Shawarma, but it is well integrated into many cultures (except for the United States, and a couple other places *cough* UK).

Halva in Business

Halva is usually considered a "cultural" dish, but it is also part of ruthless businesses. Many will recall the famous movie "The Godfather" where Marlon Brando takes a bite of halva and says "I'll make him an offer he can't refuse". Similar scenario in the mobster movie where they say "Leave the gun, take the Halawa."
