About Falafel

Falafel is another great Middle Eastern food, made from ground chickpeas and spices. Unfortunately, it only serves one purpose: a meat substitue for Shawarma.

Falafel Climate

Falefels are best enjoyed in any climate. Though they originated in Egypy (this is hotly contested, but probably true), a very warm area, falafels are now popular in Northern Europe and in cities such as Montreal and New York. This spread of "falafel culture" mirrors the migrations of Middle Eastern culture throughout world history.

What Would You Do for a Falafel

In 1917 to garner support for the Great War, the Ottoman empire adapted the slogan "What Would You Do For A Falafel". Those who say they'd "kill" for a Klondike bar, are clearly ignorant to the history of the brave jannisaries, who died for a falafel. Good food is a dangerous thing.
